üöÄ Getting Started

1

Tap the + button

On the Home screen, tap the green "+" button in the bottom-right corner to start a new propagation entry.

2

Enter plant details

Give your propagation a name (e.g., "Kitchen Pothos"), select the plant species, and choose the propagation method (water, soil, moss, etc.).

3

Add a photo

Take a photo of your fresh cutting or choose one from your library. This will be your "Day 1" reference photo.

4

Set a reminder (optional)

Enable reminders to get notified when it's time to check on your cutting, change water, or take a progress photo.

5

Save & track

Hit "Save" and your propagation will appear on the Home screen. Come back any time to add updates!

1

Active propagations

Your Home screen shows all active propagation projects as cards. Each card displays the plant name, days since start, current status, and the most recent photo.

2

Status indicators

Cards are color-coded: green for healthy, yellow for needs attention, and a checkmark for successfully rooted propagations.

3

Quick actions

Swipe on a card to access quick actions: add a photo, log a measurement, or mark as complete.

Use the "Add Entry" button to create a new journal entry. You can take a photo directly or choose from your library.

3

Add notes

Write observations like "first roots visible" or "new leaf unfurling." These notes make your timeline more informative.

Collect enough photos

üìä Growth Tracking

1

Open the Growth Tracker

Inside any propagation detail view, switch to the "Growth" tab to see the measurement tracker.

2

Log measurements

Add journal entries with notes and photos. Each entry is automatically date-stamped and contributes to your growth activity chart.

3

View charts

Watch your growth charts come to life as you add more data points. See trends, compare progress, and identify what conditions work best.

üîî Reminders & Notifications

1

Enable notifications

When prompted, allow Rootling to send notifications. You can also enable this in iOS Settings ‚Üí Rootling ‚Üí Notifications.

2

Set per-propagation reminders

Open any propagation and tap the bell icon. Choose from preset schedules (daily, every 3 days, weekly) or set a custom interval.

3

Choose reminder types

Get reminded to take photos, change water, check for roots, or mist your cuttings. Customize what each notification says.

‚òÅÔ∏è iCloud Sync

1

Go to Settings

Open Rootling and navigate to Settings (gear icon).

2

Toggle iCloud Sync

Under the Premium section, enable "iCloud Sync." This requires a Rootling Premium subscription.

3

Wait for initial sync

Your existing data will begin syncing to iCloud. This may take a few minutes depending on how much data you have.

4

Sign in on other devices

Install Rootling on your other Apple devices. As long as you're signed into the same Apple ID, your data will sync automatically.

üì± Home Screen Widget

1

Enter jiggle mode

Long-press on an empty area of your Home Screen until the apps start jiggling.

Tap the "+" button in the top-left corner of the screen to open the widget gallery.

Type "Rootling" in the search bar at the top of the widget gallery. You'll see small and medium widget sizes available.

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

Is Rootling available on the App Store?
Yes! Rootling is available on the App Store. You can track unlimited propagations, add photos, set reminders, and more. A Premium subscription unlocks additional features like iCloud sync, advanced analytics, time-lapse export, and the full plant library.
What propagation methods does Rootling support?
Rootling supports 15 propagation methods including water propagation, soil propagation, sphagnum moss, perlite, LECA, air layering, division, seed starting, leaf cuttings, stem cuttings, grafting, tissue culture, root cuttings, runners, and offsets.
Can I use Rootling on my iPad?
Yes! Rootling is a universal app that works on both iPhone and iPad. On iPad, you'll enjoy a larger layout optimized for the bigger screen.
How do I back up my data?
There are two ways: (1) Enable iCloud Sync in Settings to keep a cloud backup, or (2) your data is included in standard iPhone backups via iTunes/Finder or iCloud device backups.
My iCloud sync isn't working. What should I do?
First, check that iCloud is enabled in iOS Settings ‚Üí Apple ID ‚Üí iCloud. Make sure you have enough iCloud storage space. Try toggling sync off and on in Rootling Settings. If the issue persists, check Apple's System Status page or contact us.
Can I export my propagation data?
Yes. You can export individual propagation entries including photos and measurements, or create time-lapse videos from your photo journal. Use the share button within any propagation detail view.
Does Rootling collect my personal data?
No. Rootling is designed with privacy at its core. All your data stays on your device (or in your private iCloud if sync is enabled). We don't collect analytics, usage data, or personal information. Read our full Privacy Policy.
How do I cancel my Premium subscription?
Subscriptions are managed through Apple. Go to iOS Settings ‚Üí Apple ID ‚Üí Subscriptions ‚Üí Rootling ‚Üí Cancel. You'll continue to have Premium access until the end of your current billing period.
I have a suggestion for a new feature. How can I share it?
We'd love to hear from you! Send your ideas to mip@gmx.biz or use the "Send Feedback" option in the app's Settings. We read every suggestion.
Does the widget show real data?
Yes! The Rootling widget displays live data from your propagation projects, including the number of active propagations and recent success counts. Add it to your Home Screen from the widget gallery.

Still have questions? Contact our support team.
